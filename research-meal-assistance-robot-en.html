Research on the Development of Meal Assistance Robot Systems

This research develops meal assistance robot systems for care and home environments by integrating food recognition, mouth recognition, manipulator control, delivery mechanisms, and psychological evaluation.

Overview

Overview

Japan faces growing demand for meal assistance because of population aging and shortages of caregivers. Meal assistance is both time-consuming and physically demanding, making it an important target for robotic support.

This research has evolved from systems designed to use commercially available lunch boxes to more advanced meal assistance robots capable of recognizing, acquiring, and delivering solid food while considering user acceptance.

System Design

System Design

Recognition and Control

The system integrates ROS, MATLAB, a RealSense camera, and a multi-joint manipulator to detect food items and the user’s mouth and to perform controlled delivery.

Food Acquisition and Delivery

A combination of toothpick piercing and spoon-based delivery is used for stable handling of solid food items.

Dedicated Transfer Mechanism

A dedicated transfer mechanism is introduced near the user’s mouth so that the main manipulator does not directly approach the face, improving operational safety and psychological comfort.

Psychological Evaluation

Different delivery trajectories and offering motions are compared through user evaluation to examine perceived safety, predictability, and comfort.

Evaluation

Evaluation and Development

From 2023 to 2026, this project progressed from basic system integration and visual recognition to end-effector design, transfer mechanism development, and psychological assessment. Future work will focus on practical deployment, user acceptance, and caregiver burden reduction in real care settings.
