Research on the Development of Meal Assistance Robot Systems

This research develops meal assistance robot systems for care and home environments by integrating food recognition, mouth recognition, manipulator control, food delivery mechanisms, and psychological evaluation.

Background

Background

As Japan faces rapid population aging and shortages of caregivers, meal assistance has become one of the more demanding tasks in care and home support. For people with upper-limb impairment or those who require continuous support, robotic systems that can safely assist eating are increasingly important.

Existing support devices still face limitations in flexibility of food recognition, reliability of handling, and consideration of user psychology. This research therefore approaches meal assistance from both engineering functionality and human acceptance.

Method

Method

Recognition and Control

The system integrates ROS, MATLAB, a RealSense camera, and a multi-joint manipulator to coordinate food recognition, mouth recognition, and delivery control.

Food Acquisition and Delivery

We study end-effector designs including toothpick piercing and spoon-based delivery for stable acquisition and transfer of solid food items.

Dedicated Transfer Module

A dedicated transfer module near the user’s mouth reduces the need for the main manipulator to approach the face directly, improving psychological comfort and safety.

Psychological Evaluation

Different offering trajectories and strategies are compared to analyze perceived safety, predictability, and comfort.

Experiment

Experimental Design

The project proceeds through staged experiments, beginning with basic system integration using commercially available lunch boxes and extending to visual recognition, end-effector design, mouth delivery, dedicated transfer modules, and user evaluations.

Recognition accuracy, transfer success rate, motion stability, and subjective user impressions are evaluated together in order to compare different mechanisms and control strategies under realistic support scenarios.

Significance

Significance

The goal of this work is to establish a practical meal assistance robot system for care and home environments. Its significance lies not only in improving robotic mechanisms and control, but also in reducing caregiver burden while increasing independence and psychological comfort for users.
