Research Topic Human-Friendly Intelligent Machine Behavior

日本語 English 中文

Key Factors for Intelligent Machine Behavior that Feels Safe and Psychologically Comfortable to Humans

This research explores how intelligent machines can behave in ways that are predictable, familiar, and psychologically acceptable to humans, with a focus on structure design, motion generation, and human perception.

Background

Background

In intelligent machine design, structural configuration, including degrees of freedom, is one of the key factors that determines whether a machine can be naturally accepted by humans. When machines are expected to share the same space with people and support daily tasks such as carrying and delivering objects, functionality alone is not enough; predictability, familiarity, and affinity are also essential.

For this reason, we regard intelligent machines as natural extensions of familiar tools and devices rather than completely separate and unfamiliar technical entities. If users must consciously adapt themselves to a robot, the resulting learning burden may hinder desirable human-machine coexistence.

Method

Method

Our approach is inspired by the properties of human motion. Unlike traditional industrial robots that mainly prioritize precision, speed, and efficiency, intelligent machines designed for human environments must also be compatible with human rhythm, safety, psychological comfort, and softness of motion.

We therefore analyze the smoothness, coordination, and suppression of undesirable motion components seen in human movement, extract the motion factors that affect human perception and psychological response, and map those factors into the structural and control design of intelligent machines.

Experiment

Experimental Design

The experimental design consists of two linked stages. First, high-quality human movement is quantitatively measured and modeled. Second, the extracted characteristics are introduced into machine motion generation and compared before and after integration.

Trajectory smoothness, predictability, and coordination are evaluated together with subjective user impressions such as reassurance, familiarity, and comfort, allowing us to build an evaluation framework that reflects both engineering performance and human experience.

Significance

Significance

The ultimate goal is to establish a motion generation method that combines engineering capability with human friendliness. This work provides design principles for future human-machine coexistence in homes, healthcare, welfare, and public environments, while reducing learning cost, psychological burden, and the sense of unfamiliarity people may feel toward intelligent machines.
