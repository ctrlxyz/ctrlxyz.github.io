Research TopicHuman-Friendly Intelligent Machine Behavior

Key Factors for Intelligent Machine Behavior that Feels Safe and Psychologically Comfortable to Humans

This research explores the design conditions for intelligent machines that can coexist with humans in a psychologically acceptable and reassuring manner, with a focus on structure design, motion generation, and perception.

Concept

Design Concept

In intelligent machine systems, structural design—including degrees of freedom—is one of the key elements. Machines that share space with humans for carrying and delivering daily objects must be acceptable, familiar, and behaviorally predictable.

We therefore consider intelligent machines as natural extensions of familiar tools and devices rather than entirely separate and unfamiliar entities. Lowering the learning cost and psychological burden is essential for human-machine coexistence.

Motion

Motion Characteristics

While industrial robots have traditionally prioritized precision, speed, and efficiency, intelligent machines for human environments must also adapt to human rhythm, safety, psychological comfort, and softness of motion.

Our research draws inspiration from human movement, which is typically smooth, coordinated, and often perceived as dynamically beautiful. Such properties have value not only physically but also perceptually.

In particular, the ability of human motion to suppress undesirable components such as jitter and discontinuity suggests important design principles for human-friendly intelligent machines.

Goal

Research Goals

  • Quantitative analysis of smooth and high-quality human motion characteristics
  • Extraction of motion factors affecting perception and psychological response
  • Mapping of these factors to intelligent machine structure and control design

The ultimate goal is to establish a motion generation method that combines engineering performance with human friendliness.
