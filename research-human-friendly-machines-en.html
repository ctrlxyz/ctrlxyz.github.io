Key Factors for Intelligent Machine Behavior that Feels Safe and Psychologically Comfortable to Humans

A study on the structural design and motion generation of intelligent machines that can coexist with humans in a more natural, friendly, and psychologically acceptable way.

We believe that there are several important factors involved here, and the structure of an intelligent machine, including its degrees of freedom, is one of them. For example, if we imagine a personal intelligent machine that stays in the same room as a human being and performs tasks such as carrying and handing over everyday objects, then in order for that machine to feel more familiar and approachable to people, it is important to begin from a form that is as simple as possible and whose generated motion is easy to predict. In other words, such a machine should be positioned as an extension of the tools and devices already used in daily life. If intelligence and functionality are added little by little to tools and devices with which people are already familiar, users may one day realize that they have naturally become adept at using them. If a so-called “robot” causes people to be tense, to overthink its presence, or to require a great deal of effort in order to use it properly, then the gap between those who are interested in robots and those who are not, as well as the gap between younger and older users, will become larger, and such a situation cannot be called an ideal relationship of coexistence. We believe that coexistence should begin with “intelligent machines” that exist as an extension of familiar tools.

Another aspect that we pay close attention to is the motion trajectory generated by intelligent machines. Traditionally, intelligent machines represented by robots have mainly worked in spaces isolated from humans, such as assembly lines inside factories. For such machines, it has long been considered important to operate accurately, quickly, and efficiently. However, for the intelligent machines that are expected to coexist with people in the future, we believe that they should not move with such a severe or mechanical character. Instead, they should be considerate of people, adapt to the human pace, and move with a gentle expression.

How can an intelligent machine generate movement that is considerate of people, synchronized with the human pace, and expressed in a gentle way?

As one answer to this question, we focused on the smooth and excellent motion characteristics generated by human beings. Outstanding human movement can refresh and uplift the hearts of observers, just as people are moved when watching the smooth and dynamic performances of great athletes in Olympic broadcasts. Human motion also has a unique smoothness, together with an ability to suppress undesirable elements that disturb that smoothness. In order to apply such excellent human motion characteristics to intelligent machines, we first analyze smooth and skillful human movement in detail, extract the factors that influence human feelings and perception, and then study how those factors can be adapted to the structure and form of intelligent machines.
