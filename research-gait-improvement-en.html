Research on Functional Electrical Stimulation Gait Assistance for Knee Osteoarthritis

This research investigates a functional electrical stimulation based gait assistance system for improving walking performance and reducing joint load in patients with knee osteoarthritis.

Background

Background

In Japan’s super-aging society, musculoskeletal disorders are a major cause of support and nursing care needs. Knee osteoarthritis is especially common and is associated with varus deformity, lateral instability, pain, and deteriorated gait function. Reduced activity and lower-limb muscle weakness further accelerate disease progression.

Conventional aids such as canes, knee braces, and insoles mainly provide passive support. They do not actively intervene in muscle function or directly promote better gait patterns, which motivates the development of engineering-based active assistance methods.

Method

Method

The proposed method applies functional electrical stimulation (FES) to muscles around the knee at appropriate gait phases in order to assist muscle activity during stance and guide walking toward a more desirable motion pattern. Three-dimensional motion analysis and force plates are used to quantify knee varus angle, external knee adduction moment, ground reaction force, and multiple gait parameters.

By synchronizing active stimulation with biological muscle contraction timing, the system aims to reduce knee joint load while improving gait control and possibly enhancing muscle function.

Experiment

Experimental Design

The study first refines the device and verifies safety with healthy participants, followed by walking experiments with patients with knee osteoarthritis. Joint angles, joint moments, ground reaction forces, and gait parameters such as stride length, cadence, and support time are comprehensively evaluated to validate the proposed stimulation control strategy.

The research also considers the applicability of the device during phases surrounding total knee arthroplasty, where lower-limb alignment changes markedly in a short period.

Significance

Significance

The final goal is to develop a compact, lightweight, portable gait assistance device suitable for home use. Such a system could support continuous walking training, reduce the burden of frequent hospital visits, and contribute to care prevention and welfare engineering for patients with knee osteoarthritis.
