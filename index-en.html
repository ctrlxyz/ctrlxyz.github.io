Building human-centered intelligent control systems for the real world

Our laboratory focuses on control engineering, intelligent control, robotics, human-machine interaction, and medical and welfare engineering. We develop precise, deployable intelligent control systems and explore their practical applications in real environments.

Research Themes

Research Themes

Gait Improvement for Patients with Knee Osteoarthritis

We study gait analysis, motion assistance, and rehabilitation support methods for knee osteoarthritis to achieve safer and more natural walking improvement.

View Details

Development of a Gaze-Input Robot System for Remote Learning Participation Support

We integrate gaze input, interactive robotics, and participation support mechanisms to improve engagement, accessibility, and interaction in remote learning environments.

View Details

Key Factors for Intelligent Machine Behavior that Feels Safe and Psychologically Comfortable to Humans

We investigate how intelligent machines can behave in ways that feel reassuring, natural, and acceptable to people through structure design, motion generation, and psychological evaluation.

View Details

Research on the Development of Meal Assistance Robot Systems

We study meal assistance robots for care and home settings by combining food and mouth recognition, manipulator control, delivery mechanisms, and psychological evaluation.
