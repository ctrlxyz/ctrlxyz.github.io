Development of a Gaze-Input Robot System for Remote Learning Participation Support

This project develops a remote classroom participation support system that enables students with long-term absence due to illness or disability to participate in class from hospital rooms or home using gaze input and an avatar robot.

Background

Background

In Japan, the number of children and students unable to attend school for long periods because of serious illness or injury has been increasing. Existing support mechanisms such as hospital classes and home-visit education are important, but they face limitations in coverage, interactivity, and opportunities for social participation.

Therefore, remote education systems are needed not only to deliver learning content but also to preserve classroom presence, interaction, and social development.

System Concept

System Concept

The proposed system allows a student to control an avatar robot in the classroom through gaze input from a hospital or home environment. It is designed to provide a low-burden interface, realistic classroom participation, and timely awareness of the learner’s state by the teacher.

Method

Method

Single-Camera Gaze Input

A low-cost gaze-input method based on an ordinary web camera and PC is used to estimate the learner’s gaze direction.

Avatar Robot Control

The estimated gaze direction is used to control an omnidirectional classroom robot and its onboard camera so that the user can obtain desired classroom viewpoints.

Robust Gaze Estimation

Particle filtering is introduced to improve robustness against eyelid occlusion and small involuntary eye movements, and to support users wearing glasses.

Learning Support Mechanism

The system can automatically detect teacher pointing gestures and highlight important areas such as key regions on a blackboard in order to reduce cognitive burden.

Significance

Significance

This system is applicable not only to long-term absent students but also to remote education in broader contexts such as pandemic-related learning. It contributes to richer interaction and stronger immersion in remote education.
