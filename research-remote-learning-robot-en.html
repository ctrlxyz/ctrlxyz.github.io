Research Topic Remote Learning Support with Gaze-Input Robot

Development of a Gaze-Input Robot System for Remote Learning Participation Support

This research develops a remote learning participation support system for students who cannot attend school for long periods because of illness or disability, using gaze input and an avatar robot to enable classroom participation.

Background

Background

In Japan, the number of children and students who cannot attend school for long periods because of serious illness or injury has been increasing. Existing support mechanisms such as hospital classes and home-visit education are valuable, but they still face limitations in coverage, interactivity, learning opportunities, and support for social development.

Remote education systems are therefore expected not only to deliver educational content but also to preserve classroom presence, two-way interaction, and meaningful participation.

Method

Method

Single-Camera Gaze Input

A low-cost input method using an ordinary web camera and PC is adopted to estimate the learner’s gaze direction with minimal physical burden.

Avatar Robot Control

The estimated gaze information is used to control an omnidirectional classroom robot and its onboard camera so that the learner can actively obtain desired viewpoints.

Robust Gaze Estimation

Particle filtering and related techniques are introduced to improve robustness against eyelid occlusion, involuntary eye movement, and the use of glasses.

Lesson Understanding Support

Teacher pointing gestures are recognized to highlight important areas such as blackboard content, reducing cognitive burden for the learner.

Experiment

Experimental Design

The experiments evaluate interaction effectiveness, gaze estimation accuracy, robot control stability, and the quality of classroom participation. Technical verification is conducted for gaze input and robot operation, followed by classroom-oriented user studies focusing on presence, burden, and interaction.

In addition, the effectiveness of teacher gesture recognition and highlighted instructional cues is examined in relation to learning support and reduced cognitive load.

Significance

Significance

The goal of this research is to build a remote learning participation support system that combines accessibility, interaction, and immersion for long-term absent students. Its significance lies not only in promoting educational equity, but also in providing a human-centered and practical framework for remote education in diverse real-world situations.
